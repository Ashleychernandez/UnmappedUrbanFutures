LA Metro Light Rail and the Politics of Housing and Transit Mobility

Overview

This project examines how the expansion of Los Angeles’ light rail system into East Los Angeles has reshaped the politics of neighborhood change. While transit investments are often framed as improving connectivity and opportunity, this research investigates how they also function as catalysts for rising land values, displacement pressures, and new forms of urban inequality. Drawing on oral histories, planning documents, and a large archive of media coverage, the project traces how community organizations in neighborhoods like Boyle Heights have engaged, contested, and redefined transit-oriented development. Rather than treating infrastructure as neutral, the study positions transit agencies as key actors in regional development, whose decisions profoundly shape housing markets and community stability. At the same time, it highlights how grassroots coalitions have linked transportation justice to housing justice, advancing alternative visions of equitable development grounded in community control and accountability.
