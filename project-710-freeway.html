The 710 Freeway and Community Resistance

Overview

This project investigates the decades-long struggle over the proposed extension of the 710 Freeway through East Los Angeles, a conflict that reveals how infrastructure planning can both produce and be transformed by community resistance. For over half a century, residents of neighborhoods like El Sereno and South Pasadena have mobilized against the project, challenging state-led visions of mobility that threatened displacement, environmental harm, and neighborhood destruction. Using archival research and oral histories, the project reconstructs how this prolonged conflict reshaped regional planning debates and governance practices. It examines how community coalitions developed strategies that linked environmental justice, housing stability, and political accountability, ultimately halting a major infrastructure project. By tracing both the proposal and its non-realization, the research highlights how “unbuilt” infrastructure offers critical insight into power, refusal, and the possibilities of alternative urban futures.
