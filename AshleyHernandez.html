Faculty Profile

Ashley Hernandez, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor, Department of City and Regional Planning, UNC Chapel Hill

View Google Scholar Profile

Ashley Hernández is an Assistant Professor in the Department of City and Regional Planning at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Her research examines housing mobility, community development, and anti-displacement strategies in historically marginalized neighborhoods.

Drawing on ethnography, archival research, and spatial analysis, her work explores how racialized real estate systems shape neighborhood inequality and how community-based organizations, tenant movements, and grassroots coalitions build power to steward land and secure long-term stability. Her scholarship connects planning practice to broader questions of race, migration, urban governance, and territorial belonging. Through her teaching and research collaborations, she works closely with students and community partners to advance more equitable, community-led approaches to housing and neighborhood development.

Research

Current research areas include anti-gentrification organizing, land and housing policy, transportation-led neighborhood change, and community land strategies. Her projects examine how planning tools can reinforce inequality, and how residents build alternative governance practices in response.

Methods include interviews, oral histories, archival work, and participatory mapping. This mixed-method approach helps connect lived experience with policy and institutional analysis.
